How to Join a Club in Carlow

A simple step-by-step guide for international students joining from abroad.

🔎
Browse & Choose a Club
Use this guide to explore all available activities. Tap any activity to see the clubs nearby. Use the age filter to find clubs suitable for your age group.
📝
Pre-Register Online
Fill in our Pre-Arrival Registration Form before you travel to Ireland. We will connect you with the right club and send you everything you need to know.
📧
Get Confirmation Email
After registering, you'll receive a confirmation email with details about your chosen club, contact info, and what to expect on your first visit.
🏃
Attend Your First Session
On your first visit, arrive a few minutes early. Wear comfortable clothes if it is an active club. Irish clubs are very friendly and welcoming to newcomers.
🎉
Become a Member
Most clubs will ask you to fill a short membership form. Many clubs are free or low cost. National body clubs may have an annual fee — usually €20–€60 per year.
📌 Important Note for International Students
You do NOT need to speak Irish (Gaeilge) to join any club. English is used in all club activities. Most clubs welcome new members at any time of year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from international students about joining clubs in Carlow.

Many clubs are free or very low cost. Some sports clubs charge €20–€80 per year. Always enquire — it is always free to ask.
Yes! Most clubs accept new members throughout the year. You do not need to wait for a new season or term.
For sports: comfortable athletic clothes, trainers, and water. For arts or music: just yourself. Always arrive a few minutes early.
Carlow Town is compact and most clubs are reachable by bus, bicycle, or on foot. The Club Map shows each location so you can plan your journey.
Most clubs welcome young people from ages 5 to 26. Some clubs start as young as age 4 (like gymnastics and dance). Use the age filter on the home page to find clubs for your age group.

What International Members Say

Hear from young people who moved to Carlow and found a home through sport and community.

🇧🇷
I was so nervous when I arrived in Ireland. I didn't know anyone. Then I joined Éire Óg GAA and within a week I had 20 new friends. Hurling is amazing — I had never even heard of it before! The coaches made me feel so welcome even though my English wasn't great.
Lucas Silva
From Brazil · Joined Éire Óg GAA Club
🇵🇱
My parents were worried about me moving to a small town, but Carlow is actually full of things to do. I joined St Abban's Athletics and the Vault Youth Café. Athletics helped me stay fit and the youth café gave me a safe place to hang out. I really recommend it to anyone coming here.
Maja Kowalska
From Poland · Joined St Abban's Athletics
🇫🇷
I love music and was so happy to find Carlow Comhaltas. Traditional Irish music is beautiful and the people are so kind. They let me play my guitar at sessions even though I was still learning the Irish tunes. I also go to Spotlight Dance — it's like having two families now.
Amine Diallo
From France · Joined Carlow Comhaltas & Spotlight Dance
🇪🇸
Coming from Spain, I missed my football team back home. But Carlow Town FC welcomed me from day one. The level is good, the training is fun, and we travel to matches together on weekends. It made the whole experience of living abroad so much easier.
Pablo Martínez
From Spain · Joined Carlow Town FC
